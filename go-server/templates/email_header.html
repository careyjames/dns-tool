{{icon "graduation-cap" "me-2 text-danger"}}Understanding This Attack Security Education

This email demonstrates a growing attack pattern that defeats traditional email authentication. Here is how it works and why it slipped through:

{{icon "check-circle" "icon-2x text-success"}}
Step 1: Legitimate Infrastructure

The attacker uses a real email service (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, etc.) to send the email. Because it genuinely comes from that service, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC all pass. The infrastructure is authentic.

{{icon "mask" "icon-2x text-warning"}}
Step 2: Weaponized Content

The subject line is crafted with obfuscated brand names (using look-alike characters), fake payment amounts, and phone numbers with letter-for-digit substitution — all designed to bypass automated filters while fooling human readers.

{{icon "phone-slash" "icon-2x text-danger"}}
Step 3: The Trap

The goal is to make you panic about a fake charge and call the fraudulent phone number. The “support” agent will then ask for remote access to your computer or your payment details. Never call numbers from unsolicited emails.

{{icon "lightbulb" "me-1 text-warning"}}Key takeaway: Email authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) was designed to verify who sent it, not what it says. When the sender IS a legitimate service, authentication will always pass. Defense requires analyzing the content — which is exactly what this tool now does.
